About SFO
SFO Project is an open-source series of developed and validated Computational Fluid Dynamics (CFD) simulations implemented in Star-CCM+, Fluent & Openfoam (SFO). They are most commonly used commercial and noncommercial CFD softwares in fields engineering and science.

The step by step CFD tutorials to develop a CFD simulations for a various problems are available here and there on the web and portals of CFD software providers. However, the common denominator between all these available tutorial resources is the lack of fundamental and methodological approach to develop a CFD simulations and discussion on extended correct application or modifications of the CFD simulation. Therefore, in the best case scenario users end up with only one successful run of the simulation and numerous question marks about how to move forward to develop CFD simulation for theiractual problem of interest!

The idea and goal of the SFO Project is very simple; It wants to fill this existing large gap via presenting a purely scientific methodological approach for CFD simulation development for a problem of interest and bring all of these case studies under the same sharable and modifiable (i.e. GitHub) roof. With this approach in the end users will not only have a successful and validated CFD simulation in hand, but also have a expanded vision on how to modify the current simulation or apply it to their actual problem of interest.
